Appendix 3 — The Corporate Map
Entity Network, Address Clusters, and the Durban Resignation as Structural Marker

The corporate record of the Banksy enterprise is public. Every entity named in this appendix is registered at Companies House or an equivalent public registry. Every incorporation date, director appointment, resignation, and registered address cited here is derived from public filings. The map is not an interpretation of hidden information. It is a reading of visible information that has not previously been assembled in one place.1
What makes the corporate record evidentially significant is not any single filing but the pattern of relationships across filings.2 Entities that share directors, registered addresses, incorporation dates, or dissolution dates are entities that share operational relationships. The clustering of those relationships around a small number of individuals — and the concentration of resignations and dissolutions around two specific dates — is the corporate record's answer to the question of how the enterprise was structured and when it changed.
The Founding Layer: 1997–1998
Turtleneck Ltd3 is the earliest entity in the map with confirmed connections to the enterprise's principals. Incorporated 3 September 1997. SIC 82990.4 Founding members: Damien Hirst (50 shares, Devon address), Keith Allen (50 shares, NW3), Simon Jonathan Kennedy (50 shares, W10 — identity unconfirmed), Alex James (50 shares, WC2), Joe Strummer (50 shares, Somerset), Karen Jayde Milner (1 share, subsequently transferred to Hirst). The transfer of Milner's share to Hirst gave him effective majority control. The founding membership describes the intersection of the British art world, music industry, and film-adjacent creative network that constituted the social infrastructure of the enterprise's founding partners.
One year later, the operational layer begins. Pro-Actif Communications5 is incorporated 22 October 1998 as Identity Crisis Ltd — a name that did not survive two weeks before being changed to Pro-Actif on 11 November 1998. Still active. Darlington address. SIC 58190. The Steve Parkin connection places this entity inside the publishing and promotional infrastructure associated with the Hack interest. The name change within the first fortnight of incorporation is the earliest instance in the record of the enterprise's characteristic practice of renaming entities to obscure their function and history.
GBFTCU Ltd6 completes the 1998 founding layer. Incorporated as Electric Earth Records 12 November 1998 — three weeks after Pro-Actif. Renamed Underwater Records 14 May 2002 — the same month as the contractual lock. Renamed GBFTCU 13 November 2007 — at the point of the enterprise's commercial maturation. Dissolved 16 July 2010. The renaming sequence is a timeline of the entity's functional transitions encoded in its name history.
The Address Clusters
Three address clusters organise the operational entities of the enterprise. Each cluster corresponds to a functional tier. The clustering reflects the use of shared registered office services that place related entities at common addresses for administrative convenience and, secondarily, for the opacity that comes from having multiple entities at a single address.
Maddox Street W1S 1QP7 is the POW cluster. Pictures on Walls, Dismaland Ltd, Paranoid Pictures, GDP (Gross Domestic Product), and Outline Design are all registered here. This is the publishing and production tier — the entities that interface directly with the commercial output of the enterprise. Mayfair address. The geographic choice is consistent with the enterprise's positioning as a fine art commercial operation rather than a street art project.
New Street Square EC4A 3AQ8 is the Hirst cluster. MurderMe Ltd (subsequently Prints and Editions Ltd) and Other Criteria Ltd are registered here. The Hirst print publishing and merchandising operation was administered from a separate address cluster to the POW operation. The formal corporate distance between the AoR's vehicles and the publisher is a structural feature of the enterprise, not an accident of administration. It provided legal separation between the Hirst-facing entities and the Banksy-facing entities that was useful in the event of scrutiny.
Old Gloucester Street WC1N 3AX9 is the PCO cluster. Pest Control Office Ltd and BBAY Art Ltd (incorporated October 2019, dissolved January 2026) share this address. The PCO and BBAY Art at the same registered address is the corporate record's clearest statement of their operational relationship. An authentication body and a secondary market advisory vehicle sharing a registered address are not independent entities operating in parallel. They are components of an integrated secondary market management operation.
The BBAY Network
The BBAY cluster10 is the most complex sub-network in the corporate map. BBAY Ltd, active 2009–2017, is the primary vehicle through the peak bubble years. Its successor dormant entity, Many Hands Home Help, was dissolved October 2020. BBAY (Bushey) Ltd (07311363, incorporated July 2010, Wood Green, SIC 68100 real estate) remains active. BBAY Art Ltd (12281596, incorporated October 2019 at the PCO address, dissolved January 2026) is the secondary market advisory vehicle whose dissolution coincides with the filing of the Ant and Dec High Court proceedings.
The BBAY property group11 extends to thirteen entities spanning 2008–2026. The core cluster was incorporated in August–October 2008 — the same period as the Moonstar incorporation in Florida. The Pall Mall pair was dissolved on the same day in January 2016. The Clapham entity was dissolved in December 2017 with an insolvency event. Stamford UK Holdings (formerly BBAY Properties) was renamed on 20 February 2023 — nineteen days after a Jersey entity, BBAY Eaton Place, was registered on the UK overseas companies register.
The nineteen-day gap between the Jersey registration and the UK rename is a corporate sequencing tell. You register the offshore vehicle first, then rename the onshore vehicle to create nominal distance between the old name and the new structure. The BBAY network is a shadow secondary market infrastructure operating across onshore UK, offshore Jersey, and dormant/dissolved vehicle layers simultaneously.
The Hirst Offshore Layer
Science Ltd (Jersey) FC02927812 is the offshore intellectual property and licensing vehicle. Its previous name — Hirst Holdings Limited, held from 10 November 2009 to 12 May 2011 — places it unambiguously within the Hirst corporate family. Its UK establishment was opened 24 August 2009 at 14 Welbeck Street W1G 9XU and closed 24 February 2014. The Jersey incorporation structure is standard for holding intellectual property and licensing income outside the UK tax base. The rename from Hirst Holdings to Science Ltd in May 2011 follows the same logic as the MurderMe to Prints and Editions rename: nominal distance between the entity and its most identifiable principal.
The Lazarides Separation
LazInc13 occupies a structurally significant position in the corporate map because its separation timeline is precisely documented. Simon Durban served as LazInc secretary from February 2006 to March 2009. That March 2009 date is the Lazarides separation date — the point at which the operational connection between the Lazarides gallery operation and the enterprise's corporate core was formally severed. The Outsiders and Execution Prints entities — both components of the Lazarides secondary distribution network — were dissolved on the same day in July 2015. Coordinated same-day dissolutions are a recurring pattern in the corporate map. They are the record of deliberate, coordinated wind-downs executed by people with a shared interest in closing multiple vehicles simultaneously.
The Two Pivot Dates
The entire corporate map organises itself around two dates that function as structural pivots.
7 November 201914 is the first pivot. On this single date, Simon Durban resigned simultaneously from POW, PCO, Paranoid Pictures, Dismaland Ltd, and GDP — five of the enterprise's core operating entities. Five simultaneous resignations by the individual who had served as the operational connective tissue of the enterprise since 2005 is not a routine administrative event. It is a controlled demolition of the first-generation corporate structure.
January 202015 is the second pivot. PCO was acquired by POW. MurderMe was renamed Prints and Editions. Both events in the same week. The acquisition completed the vertical integration of the enterprise: the authentication body came under the ownership of the publisher, eliminating the formal independence of the certification function. The gap between the two pivots is approximately ten weeks — the transition period in which the enterprise operated without its full corporate architecture before the consolidated second-generation structure was in place.

What the corporate map establishes, read as a complete document, is the full structural anatomy of a twenty-year commercial operation. Every date in this map is a public record. The map was always available to anyone who looked for it. This appendix is the record of having looked.

Character Summaries
The Principals of the Enterprise: Function, Biography, and Evidentiary Position

The summaries that follow describe each principal's function within the enterprise, the evidentiary basis for that characterisation, and their current position in the record as of March 2026.25 They are portraits derived from evidence, not speculation. Where inference is required it is stated as inference. Where the record is definitive it is stated as definitive.
Lucy McKenzie — The Artist
Hand. Trompe L'Oeil Painter. Creative Executor.
Born Glasgow, 1977.26 Trained at Duncan of Jordanstone College of Art, Dundee. Career based between Glasgow, Brussels, and New York. Represented by Galerie Buchholz and Cabinet Gallery. Her practice is trompe l'oeil — the production of painted surfaces that deceive the eye into reading them as something other than hand-applied paint.27
The technical argument is simple. The Banksy graphic vocabulary requires a painter who can approximate the visual appearance of stencilled spray paint in hand-applied media. This is trompe l'oeil applied to street art iconography. It is a technically demanding practice. It is Lucy McKenzie's primary technical practice. The match between the skill the work requires and the skill she demonstrably possesses is not coincidental.
She was present in the pre-canonical network. Glasgow Peace Is Tough 2001 places her inside a documented exhibition curated by Bernie Reid — the same Reid credited on the Untitled 06 edition — one year before the May 2002 contractual lock. The show's omission from the official Banksy Exhibition record is the enterprise's acknowledgment that her presence there is something it cannot afford to have publicly connected to the Banksy timeline.
The 2005 unique — the oil-painted bouquet — is the one confirmed painterly-mode work in the canonical record. The doubling motif28 — Untitled 1 and 2, the Olympia photographs — encodes the two-person creative relationship in the formal structure of the works themselves. These are not coincidences of composition. They are a signature.
Her Super Palace show at Z33, Belgium, ran September 2024 through February 2025. High Court proceedings were filed in March 2026, within weeks of the show's close. Her evidentiary position is the strongest of any principal. She is the only individual in the record whose documented technical practice, institutional presence in the pre-canonical network, and physical proximity to the anomalous edition objects all align simultaneously with the requirements of the Artist function.
Kerri McKenzie — The Voice
Conceptual Author. Written Output. Intellectual Architecture.
Oxford physics and philosophy graduate. HPS metaphysics fundamentality PhD, University of Cambridge, 2012.29 Currently Professor of Philosophy at the University of California San Diego. Her published academic work addresses fundamentality, dependence, and the metaphysics of physical structure — the relationship between what appears at the surface and what is structurally determinative underneath.
The conceptual parallel is not decorative. The written output associated with the Banksy name is characterised by exactly the philosophical preoccupations that define Kerri McKenzie's published academic work.30 Surface and substrate. Appearance and underlying structure. The visible and the determinative. A person trained in the metaphysics of fundamentality who is also producing the conceptual architecture of a major art enterprise will leave traces of that training in the work. The traces are there.
The PhD completion in 2012 is concurrent with the Round 2 capitalisation period, the artist re-engagement sequence, and the Love Hurts VIP Round 1 allocation. A person managing two parallel careers simultaneously would complete her doctorate at precisely the moment when both careers were reaching their respective institutional peaks.
Her function is the Voice: the written Banksy persona, the conceptual framing, the intellectual architecture that gives the work its political and philosophical register. Without the Voice, the works are technically accomplished images. With the Voice, they are political statements with auction records. Her evidentiary position is strong but more inferential than Lucy's — structural and pattern-based rather than object-based. It is the right kind of evidence for the right kind of function.
Damien Hirst — The Artist of Record
Corporate Controlling Stakeholder. Legal Identity. Financial Architecture.
Born Bristol, 1965.31 YBA generation. Turner Prize 1995. Beautiful Inside My Head Forever, Sotheby's, September 2008: £111 million. Founding member of Turtleneck Ltd 1997. Controlling stake via Milner share transfer. MurderMe Ltd (subsequently Prints and Editions Ltd) and Other Criteria Ltd at New Street Square EC4A 3AQ. Science Ltd (Jersey), formerly Hirst Holdings Ltd: offshore IP vehicle.
The AoR function32 is the legal and commercial identity of the enterprise. Hirst is not characterised in this Codex as the person who produces the Banksy works or writes the Banksy texts. He is characterised as the person whose legal identity underwrites the enterprise's commercial activity, who manages the corporate architecture through which the financial returns are distributed, and who signed works when the artist could not or would not sign under standard terms.
The Wreck of the Unbelievable edition of 2 with 5 APs encodes Lucy McKenzie's share in a Hirst vehicle.33 The NAPALM/SERPENTINE 29 APs encodes the artist's post-schism condition that the AoR be excluded from participation in specific works. Both structures are contractual statements recorded in edition architecture rather than in any filing.
The schism's root is Hirst's 2009 decision to cash out investors and abandon the multi-artist diversification plan in favour of monopoly — going all-in on himself and Banksy. That decision broke the artist's walk-away deal and initiated the renegotiation sequence whose terms are visible in the post-schism edition structures. His evidentiary position is the most thoroughly documented of any principal through the corporate record.
Simon Durban — The Operator
Administrative Core. First-Generation Structure. Transition Manager.
Born August 1970.34 Thirteen Companies House appointments across the enterprise's corporate cluster. POW director 2005–7 November 2019. PCO founding director 2008–7 November 2019. Five simultaneous resignations on 7 November 2019. Post-board vehicle: Positive Accounting Solutions Ltd.
Durban is the enterprise's administrative spine for fourteen years — the connective tissue between the publishing function (POW), the authentication function (PCO), the production function (Paranoid Pictures, Dismaland), and the ancillary entities. The five simultaneous resignations on 7 November 2019 are the single most evidentially significant event in his record. You do not resign from five entities on the same day by accident. You do it when you have negotiated your departure and agreed on a clean-break date. The Positive Accounting Solutions intangible assets — £560,000 opening balance corresponding to the migrated Parkin stake — are the financial record of what he took with him.
The 2007 Girl with Balloon / Morons Sepia inscription to 'Simon' is the artist's personal acknowledgment of Durban's position — a gift that encoded the relationship in a physical object two years before the formal departure date, at the moment of structural transition.
Bernie Reid — The Connector
Pre-Canonical Network. Curatorial Infrastructure. Edition Credit.
Scottish artist and curator.35 Lead curator, Glasgow Peace Is Tough, 2001. Credited on the Untitled 06 edition — a closed edition of ten works sent from Switzerland, among the most anomalous objects in the Banksy print corpus.
His position is that of the institutional connector: the person who organised the exhibition space that placed Lucy McKenzie inside the collaborative network at the founding moment, and whose credit on the Untitled 06 edition is the physical remnant of an arrangement that predates the formal commercial architecture. His subsequent absence from the official record is the Glasgow omission in human form.
His evidentiary position is entirely object-based. The Untitled 06 credit is printed on the work. It does not require inference. It requires only that you hold the work and read what it says, then work backward to find the institutional context that explains why his name is there. That context is Glasgow Peace Is Tough. The line from the credit to the show to Lucy McKenzie to the enterprise's founding network is direct and documented.
Robin Gunningham — The False Flag
Misdirection Vehicle. Brand Frontperson. Tabloid Legend.
Bristol-based individual.36 Identified in tabloid press as 'Banksy.' The identification was accepted widely and has never been definitively refuted by the enterprise. It has also never been confirmed.
His function in this analysis is the first false flag — a real person, real enough to be found through diligent journalism, whose identity was seeded into street art networks as legend at a point when the enterprise needed the identification narrative to be absorbed and redirected. The Gunningham thesis requires the enterprise to have been built by a Bristol graffiti artist with no documented corporate experience and no institutional connection to the fine art market. The corporate record describes an enterprise built by people with access to gallery infrastructure, auction house relationships, and a legal architecture sophisticated enough to manage a twenty-year commercial operation across multiple jurisdictions. These two descriptions cannot both be true.
Holly Cushing — The Administrator
PCO Founding Officer. Candidate Party X.
PCO Secretary from January 2008 — PCO's founding date.37 PCO Director from 2013. Quiet exit 2019. Director of Slap Dash Inc (SIC 90040, incorporated June 2016, dissolved September 2025).
Cushing administered the authentication body from its founding through the first-generation wind-down. Her position gave her operational knowledge of every work that passed through PCO certification, every certificate issued, and every transaction that required authentication. The Party X characterisation in the context of the Ant and Dec proceedings is inferential. The structural candidate for Party X in the post-March 2009 separation period is the person with authenticated access to PCO certification, operational independence following the Lazarides separation, and a dissolved corporate vehicle that closed concurrent with Moonstar in September 2025. Cushing fits that structural description. This is stated as inference, not conclusion.
Lazarides — The Distributor
Street Credibility Infrastructure. Distribution Channel. Separated 2009.
Bristol photographer.38 Dazed and Confused contributor 1998–2002. Street art photography sold to Thrasher, Juxtapoz, The Face. LazInc director 2004–2020. Laz share buyback October 2008. Durban resigns LazInc secretary March 2009. Rivers Threadneedle liquidation February 2025.
His function was the street credibility infrastructure — the element of the enterprise that the corporate principals could not supply and that the enterprise required to be believed. The characterisation in this Codex is the Duddy Kravitz model: the hardscrabble working-class outsider picked by the founding partners as ringleader of the artist-facing operation. He provided what the enterprise needed, built a significant gallery business in the process, and was separated cleanly when the enterprise's structure no longer required the same street credibility intermediary function.
He has spoken more openly about the Banksy operation than any other principal. What he has said is consistent with the structural analysis in this Codex. What he has not said is consistent with the separation agreement that the October 2008 buyback implies.

These are the principals of the Banksy enterprise as this investigation has identified them. Their functions are distinct. Their biographies are documented. Their positions in the corporate record are verifiable. The investigation does not require any of them to confirm or deny what the record already shows. The record is the evidence. The summaries are the reading of it.
